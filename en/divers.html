Cinema

Along with music, cinema is another art form I deeply appreciate. My favorite films are "Die Hard" directed by John McTiernan, "Nightcrawler" by Dan Gilroy, and the recent "Yannick" by Quentin Dupieux.

These three films belong to very different genres but remain great works. The staging and camera movements of the first "Die Hard" redefined the genre until the release of "Die Hard 3", which again redefined the codes established by "Die Hard".

"Nightcrawler" is a gripping thriller that tells the story of Lou, who roams Los Angeles in search of shocking footage to sell to TV stations. I particularly appreciate Jake Gyllenhaal's performance as Lou, who becomes obsessed with finding increasingly graphic images, to the point of losing his morality.

Finally, "28 Days Later" is a striking post-apocalyptic film that reinvents the zombie genre with intensity. After a devastating virus transforms the population into enraged creatures, Jim, a young courier, awakens from a coma in a deserted and apocalyptic London. He joins a small group of survivors trying to escape the infected and the dangers lurking in this ruined world. The film stands out for its oppressive atmosphere, tense direction, and visceral portrayal of chaos. Danny Boyle skillfully blends horror and drama, enhanced by an immersive soundtrack and a grainy cinematography created by the digital cameras of the time.
